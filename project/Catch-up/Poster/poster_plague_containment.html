1Problem & Motivation

Epidemic containment requires rapid local decisions (which area to protect next) combined with global planning (how to isolate the infected zone without fragmenting healthy regions).

Plague Containment models this as a turn-based strategy game on an 8×8 city grid, where each district carries a risk level (1–10) and the plague spreads every turn.

2Algorithm Design

Greedy Vaccination

Recommends the highest-risk healthy neighbor of the infected zone for immediate vaccination.

1
Scan all infected districts and collect their healthy neighbors (no duplicates)
2
Select the candidate with the highest risk level
3
Mark it as vaccinated and return the recommendation
Why greedy? The most urgent threat is always the highest-risk unprotected neighbor. Local optimality aligns with the immediate game objective.

Backtracking Quarantine

Finds a set of districts to quarantine that fully encloses the infected zone without isolating any healthy area.

1
Identify all healthy border districts adjacent to infected cells
2
For each candidate: try quarantining it, then check connectivity
3
If connectivity breaks → backtrack (undo) and try next option
4
If full enclosure achieved with connectivity → return solution
Why backtracking? A greedy approach could accidentally isolate a healthy region. Backtracking explores all valid combinations and undoes bad choices.

Why linked list? Infection grows dynamically — we don't know how many districts will be infected. Insertions at the tail are O(1).

AVL Tree — District Risk Index

Stores districts ordered by risk level. Self-balancing guarantees O(log n) lookups for the highest-risk district.
